Misinformation researcher

Most recently, I was a senior researcher at The Information Futures Lab at Brown University. I investigated the harmful impacts of misinformation on public health and explored how we can make quality information more accessible to the public.

My latest research involved training a machine-learning classifier to analyze millions of social media posts about climate change. We found that only 3.1 percent of social media posts and news headlines about climate change also mentioned its health impacts, meaning the public rarely learns about the connection between the two -- an approach that could have helped drive support for climate action. Additionally, we found that news media predominantly covered the health impacts of climate change through purely negative messaging , without offering readers actionable solutions, ultimately missing the mark.

I am looking to focus more on data-driven, investigative journalism. For example, I recently created this artificial intelligence-powered monitoring dashboard that flags rumors about election integrity and identifies the locations mentioned in posts shared on a far-right message board, and data visualizations illustrating how the 2024 presidential election played out in New York City.

Some of my previous works on misinformation and disinformation can be found on the archive of First Draft, where I was a senior researcher and published my tracking and reporting throughout the Covid-19 pandemic and the 2020 election. You may have also come across my other writing on health misinformation, participatory disinformation, voter suppression, and other forms of information disorder, which have been cited in various publications.
